The Context

Why MARTA matters now more than ever.

A Vital Artery

The Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority (MARTA) stands as a cornerstone of Atlanta's urban infrastructure. It weaves together the diverse tapestry of neighborhoods, business districts, and cultural hubs that define the city. Yet, as the metro region projects to add over 2.5 million new residents by 2040, the current system faces a critical tipping point.

Traffic congestion already costs the region billions annually in lost productivity. The need for a robust, expanded transit network is not just a convenienceâ€”it is an economic and environmental necessity. This project explores how MARTA's expansion is the key to unlocking Atlanta's potential, moving beyond the "commuter" model to a comprehensive lifestyle network.

Historical Challenges

Since its inception in 1971, the system was envisioned as a futuristic solution to traffic and a tool for integration. However, expansion has often been curtailed by regional funding disputes and suburban resistance, creating a unique patchwork of coverage. Our project highlights these milestones to understand the flow of history.

The Vision

More than just a ride.

Transit-Oriented Development

Transforming station parking lots into vibrant mixed-use communities. It is no longer just about moving people from point A to point B; it is about creating destinations.

Strategic Expansion

Focusing on high-impact projects like the Clifton Corridor and Campbellton Road BRT. These expansions aim to connect major employment centers and residential hubs, closing the gaps in the current network.

Sustainability

Reducing reliance on automobiles and promoting a greener, more walkable city. Efficient transit is key to Atlanta's environmental goals.
