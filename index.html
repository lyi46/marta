Reimagining the Metropolitan Atlanta Rapid Transit Authority for a connected, sustainable future.

The Context

Why MARTA matters now more than ever.

A Vital Artery

For many in Metro Atlanta, MARTA is an important, viable solution for avoiding rush hour, offering cost-effective and efficient transit. Yet, as the city's population booms, MARTA's current decline in service quality is driving new commuters into their cars. This choice directly worsens traffic patterns and increases road congestion.


The cost of Atlanta's traffic is significant: drivers consume up to 105 hours and $1,500 annually just sitting in congestion. Furthermore, as commuters increasingly choose driving over mass transit, the grave environmental impact from car emissions continues to worsen.

Historical Challenges

While MARTA showed promise initially, there has been a recent slow in progress due to lack of funding and community pushback on expansion. Both Gwinett and Cobb county residents vote against expansion, which leaves many commuters in those counties without viable mass transit solutions. This forces many more drivers on the roads and increases costs of living all around.

Challenges Facing MARTA

As Atlanta's population continues to surge and the strain on our roadways is becoming more apparent, the demand for efficient transit grows. However, MARTA faces challenges in keeping pace with this rapid expansion.

The Solutions

Transforming the map with high-capacity transit.

Infrastructure Overhaul

MARTA will undergo a comprehensive upgrade of its rail and bus systems to streamline operations and reduce maintenance costs and delays.

Safety & Public Image

We will improve MARTA by focusing on two areas: upgrading security systems and increasing personnel, and modernizing stations to create a cleaner, more welcoming environment.

Lack of Expansion of Transit

Strategic MARTA expansion—specifically along the BeltLine and major highway arteries—is projected to significantly boost ridership, which will consequently alleviate traffic congestion on our roadways.
